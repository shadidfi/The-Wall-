Let Me Leave

-Abid (3028)

Whoever can, let him do it, but not me
Here’s the bloody crown, man’s highest power.
Let me leave;
Albeit with a shadow of failure.

Human civilization- let me go, I have seen enough
In the name of civilization.
No more, let me go.
Happiness, for which so much madness and greed
Pour fourth,
No- no more, let me go.
I can’t bear the helpless suicide of whales,
Evils of starving children,
Hungry bodies aging quickly,
Thin stretching hands of the Third world,
Let me go.

Right this instant,
Let me go.

Terrible agony, wasteful suffering,
Fear and weeping from refugee camps, torture,
Goodbye war, mass murder,
Goodbye, goodbye.

No more shiny weapons,
Atom bombs, neutron bombs and other nonsense,
No more sinister plots for human destruction.
Whoever can, let him do it, but not me.
I hate it all.
Satisfied even a shadow of failure,
Let me leave.
