Jester’s Privilege

-Shaiham (3171)

Visceral waves of intellect,
An ordinary find more or less.
The arrow of trout you dare deflect,
“The cap and bells”, an unswollen address.

The sharpness of a tongue well bred,
It cuts you down you bleed regret.
Had you not handed me my marotte,
One could have enjoyed their upper hand.

All in the name of amusement and jest,
Lottery of McCleary, a strange request.
You have nothing a scepter at best,
“Throw away your crown”, Just a jester’s behest.

In exchange of an elicit of a rise,
Ignorance and dismissal is my prize.
A waste of prudence lice no other.
Along the lines of revenge, meet me at the edge,
A fool’s perfect use of jester’s privilege.
